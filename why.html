The challenges
we address

Problem Faced

Inefficiency and Error-Prone :

Manually tracking and entering student records is a labor-intensive process prone to errors, making it an inefficient method for managing large volumes of data..

Risk of Data Loss :

Physical documents are susceptible to being misplaced or damaged, leading to potential loss of critical student information and complicating the retrieval of accurate records.

Complicated Transcript Management :

At the end of the school year, issuing transcripts to colleges becomes problematic if hard copies are missing, requiring time-consuming manual searches through records to recreate lost documents.

Our Focus Points

Academics :

Each student's academic record is maintained in a dossier, which must be regularly updated for report cards and scholar badges. To receive a scholar badge each year, students must submit their report cards. If a report card is lost, retrieving a copy from a previous year involves a lengthy and complicated process.

Issuing Books :

Students at school are granted a library card for issuing books but at times, the card gets loose and is lost by the student. Due to this, the track of the books is lost by the librarian. The track of the books is kept manually by the librarian which is a tedious job considering the number of students in a school. This requires a lot of manpower and man-hours

Medical Data :

Every student has a medical record written on a paper document. For every student, it becomes difficult to keep track of their medical history when they go to the medical room. If ever there is an emergency, taking out a file for that child would waste a lot of time and the medical care required by the student might not be met at the right time.

Access to shops
(in school) :

At the start of a new school session, students need to purchase new books and uniforms, often carrying a significant amount of money to do so. This money can sometimes be misplaced due to absent-mindedness. Similarly, in the canteen, students may lose money in the crowd or spend excessively without any limit, making it difficult to manage their expenses.
